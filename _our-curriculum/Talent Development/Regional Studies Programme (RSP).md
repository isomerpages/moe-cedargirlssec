<h4>About</h4>
<p>The Regional Studies Programme (RSP) is a six-year scholarship programme
offered only to five secondary schools in Singapore with a seamless progression
to three junior colleges, including Victoria Junior College. The main objective
of the programme is to nurture a group of students who are conversant in
Malay Language and sensitised to the cultural, socio-economic and political
developments of the ASEAN countries. Through the learning of Malay Special
Programme (MSP) as their third language, this enhances their understanding
and experience of cultures in the region. Our RSP students have the opportunity
to immerse themselves in authentic settings of the dynamic cultures of
our neighbouring countries through experiential learning and activities
that are conducted in Singapore or through our biennial overseas exchange
trips.</p>
<h4>Key Programs / Competitions</h4>
<p><strong><u>Year 1 Regional Studies Programme Cultural Camp</u></strong>
</p>
<p>The camp provides Year 1 RSP students with the opportunity to engage meaningfully
in Bahasa Melayu and Bahasa Indonesia through authentic cultural immersion.
By collaborating with peers from the 5 RSP schools, students deepen their
appreciation of Malay and Indonesian traditions while building strong regional
connections in a practical and interactive environment.</p>
<p><strong><u>Year 3 Regional Studies Programme Talk</u></strong>
</p>
<p>The talk is organised for the Sec 3 RSP students from the 5 RSP schools
students to enhance and extend the knowledge gained during their Sec 3
Understanding Southeast Asia (USEA) course which covers the culture and
belief systems of people in Southeast Asia and how these have shaped the
countries’ social, political and economic structures.</p>
<p><strong><u>Local Enrichment Programmes</u></strong>
</p>
<p>At different levels, the students are exposed to the different activities
and programmes aligned to the curriculum. These programmes help RSP students
deepen their knowledge of Malay language and culture through seminars,
workshops, and learning journeys. By exploring the history, politics, and
economy of the region, students gain a clearer understanding of the diverse
factors that shape Southeast Asia today.</p>
<p><strong><u>Year 2 &amp; Year 3 Overseas Immersion Trips</u></strong>
</p>
<p>The programme provides opportunities for students to immerse themselves
in authentic settings through immersion trips to Malaysia, Indonesia and
Brunei. Through interaction, hands-on activities and learning through artifacts,
our students are able to comprehend the communities from the perspective
/ lens of the native speaker beyond Singapore.</p>
