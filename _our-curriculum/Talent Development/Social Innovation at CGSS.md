<h3>SOCIAL INNOVATION @ CGSS</h3>
<h4>About</h4>
<p>The Social Innovation (SI) Programme is a niche programme that nurtures
socially conscious, future-ready learners who are equipped to make a meaningful
difference in the world. Anchored in the Design Thinking framework and
aligned with the MOE 21st Century Competencies (21CCs), the programme provides
authentic learning experiences that develop students holistically in knowledge,
skills and values.</p>
<p>Guided by the mission to empower students to become</p>
<p>a. innovative problem-solvers, b. empathetic changemakers, and c. collaborative
leaders who drive positive social change,</p>
<p>the SI Programme enables students to identify real-world community needs
and design human-centered, sustainable solutions.</p>

<p>The programme comprises two key components: Social Innovation and Research
(SIR) and Talent Development - Social Innovation (TDSI). Together, SIR
and TDSI reflect Cedar’s commitment to developing learners who are confident
in navigating complexity, responsive to community needs, and ready to contribute
meaningfully in a rapidly changing world.</p>
<h4>Social Innovation and Research (SIR)</h4>
<p>Social Innovation and Research (SIR) is a school-based curriculum offered
to the entire cohort of Year 1 and Year 2 students, structured within their
timetable. Through the iterative stages of Design Thinking—empathy, problem
definition, ideation, prototyping and testing—students develop critical
and inventive thinking, communication and collaboration skills, and a strong
sense of civic literacy and social responsibility. Learning is inquiry-driven
and experiential, allowing students to apply research skills in authentic
contexts.</p>
<p>In Year 2, students have the opportunity to collaborate with external
beneficiaries to implement their Social Innovation projects, translating
ideas into purposeful action and strengthening their understanding of community
engagement.</p>

<h4>Talent Development - Social Innovation (TDSI)</h4>
<p>Talent Development - Social Innovation (TDSI) is a tailored and more rigorous
programme for selected students who demonstrate strong interest and aptitude
in Social Innovation.</p>
<p>Through community trails, students practise empathy-building, interviewing
and needs-analysis skills, and deepen their learning through Makers Education
under MOE GEB’s Innovation Programme (IvP). They also get to participate
in national-level platforms and competitions such as the Junior Achievement
(JA) Company of the Year competition and the Community Problem-Solving
Programme (CmPS).</p>
<p>Through these experiences, students apply entrepreneurial and inventive
thinking, receive authentic feedback from mentors and partners, and iteratively
refine their solutions.</p>
