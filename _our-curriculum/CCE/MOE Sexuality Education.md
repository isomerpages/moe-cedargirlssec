<p style="margin-right:0in;margin-left:0in;font-size:20px;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,serif;margin:0in;"><strong><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">CEDAR GIRLS’ SECONDARY SCHOOL</span></strong></p>

<p style="margin-right:0in;margin-left:0in;font-size:20px;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,serif;margin:0in;"><strong><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">Moe Sexuality Education in Schools</span></strong></p>
<p style="margin-right:0in;margin-left:0in;font-size:16px;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,serif;margin:0in;"><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p style="margin-right:0in;margin-left:0in;font-size:16px;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,serif;margin:0in;"><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">1.&nbsp;</span><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">Sexuality Education (SEd) in schools aims to enable students to understand the physiological, social and emotional changes they experience as they mature, develop healthy and rewarding relationships with others, including those with members of the opposite sex, and make wise, informed and responsible decisions on sexuality matters. SEd is premised on the importance of the family as the basic unit of society. This means encouraging healthy, heterosexual marriages and stable nuclear family units with extended family support. The teaching and learning of SEd is based on respect for the values and beliefs of the different ethnic and religious communities in Singapore on sexuality issues.</span></p>
<p style="margin-right:0in;margin-left:0in;font-size:16px;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,serif;margin:0in;"><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">&nbsp;</span></p>


<p style="margin-right:0in;margin-left:0in;font-size:16px;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,serif;margin:0in;"><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">2.The <strong>Goals</strong> of Sexuality Education are to:</span></p>
<p style="margin-right:0in;margin-left:0in;font-size:16px;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,serif;margin:0in;"><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<ol style="list-style-type: undefined;margin-left:0.5in;">
    <li><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;font-size:16px">Support students in managing their physiological, social and emotional changes as they grow up and develop safe and healthy relationships.</span></li>
    <li><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;font-size:16px">Guide students to make wise, informed and responsible decisions on sexuality matters.</span></li>
    <li><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;font-size:16px">Help students develop a moral compass and respect for themselves and others by having positive mainstream values and attitudes about sexuality that are premised on the family as the basic unit of society.</span></li>
</ol>
<p style="margin-right:0in;margin-left:0in;font-size:16px;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,serif;margin:0in;"><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p style="margin-right:0in;margin-left:0in;font-size:16px;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,serif;margin:0in;"><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">3.The&nbsp;</span><strong><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">Key Messages</span></strong><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">&nbsp;of Sexuality Education are:</span></p>
<p style="margin-right:0in;margin-left:0in;font-size:16px;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,serif;margin:0in;"><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<ol style="list-style-type: undefined;margin-left:0.5in;">
    <li><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;font-size:16px">Love and respect yourself as you love and respect others;</span></li>
    <li><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;font-size:16px">Build positive relationships based on love and respect (which are the foundation for strong families);</span></li>
    <li><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;font-size:16px">Make responsible decisions for yourself, your family and society; and</span></li>
    <li><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;font-size:16px">Abstinence before marriage is the best protection against STIs/HIV and unintended pregnancies. Casual sex can harm and hurt you and your loved ones.</span></li>
</ol>

<p style="margin-right:0in;margin-left:0in;font-size:16px;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,serif;margin:0in;"><strong><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">Overview of Cedar Girls’ Secondary School’s Sexuality Education Programme for 2024</span></strong></p>
<p style="margin-right:0in;margin-left:0in;font-size:16px;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,serif;margin:0in;"><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">4.Sexuality Education is delivered in a holistic manner through the school curriculum. The content for Sexuality Education is grouped into five main themes: Human Development, Interpersonal Relationships, Sexual Health, Sexual Behaviour, and, Culture, Society and Law. You may click <a href="https://go.gov.sg/moe-sexuality-education-scope">here</a> for more information on the scope of Sexuality Education in the school curriculum.</span></p>

<ol style="list-style-type: lower-alpha;">

</ol>

<p style="margin-right:0in;margin-left:0in;font-size:16px;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,serif;margin:0in;"><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">6.The values and attitudes of our youth towards sexuality are increasingly shaped by their peers, exposure to the media, popular culture and the internet. Adolescence is a complex stage in life as the adolescent attempts to find his/her own identity and often struggles with new social relationships. The period of middle adolescence (generally from 15 – 17 years old) can be a turbulent period for adolescents. The gap between physical and emotional/cognitive maturity may result in a tendency to act on impulse, experiment with various activities (including sexual activities), sometimes without understanding the unpleasant consequences that may arise from such experimentation. The need for peer acceptance can be overwhelmingly strong and hinder one’s ability to act responsibly. &nbsp;Given the pressures faced by the middle adolescent from within themselves and from peers, it is important to build a strong sense of self-worth, personal conviction and an understanding of the possible consequences of sexual behaviour.</span></p>
<p style="margin-right:0in;margin-left:0in;font-size:16px;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,serif;margin:0in;"><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">&nbsp;</span></p>
<p style="margin-right:0in;margin-left:0in;font-size:16px;font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,serif;margin:0in;"><span style="font-family:&quot;Arial&quot;,sans-serif;">7.Through the Sexuality Education lessons, secondary students will have the opportunity to develop a positive self-esteem, develop their character, and expand their understanding and repertoire of skills to deal with various issues related to sexuality, which are of prime concern at this age.</span></p>
